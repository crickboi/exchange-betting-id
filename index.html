Betting Exchange IDs: How They Work in 2026

Exchange betting IDs work differently from regular cricket IDs, and honestly, most people don't realize how big the difference is until they try both. With an exchange, you're not betting against a bookie. You're betting against other people. That changes everything.

Exchange vs Regular Betting

Regular betting: the bookie sets the odds, you take it or leave it. Exchange betting: you can set your own odds, and someone else matches your bet. The exchange just takes a small commission.

Why does this matter? Better odds. On a typical IPL match, exchange odds are 5-10% better than what bookies offer. Over a full season, that adds up to a lot.

Getting an Exchange ID

Process is basically the same as a regular ID. WhatsApp a provider, deposit via UPI, get your credentials. The platform interface is a bit different since you'll see back and lay options, but you get used to it fast.

Quick Tip

If you're new to exchanges, start with match winner markets. The back/lay system makes more sense there. Once you're comfortable, move to session markets where the real value is. We've seen people do really well on session betting once they understand how laying works.
